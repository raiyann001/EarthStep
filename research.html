Behind the design

The research behind EarthStep.

EarthStep's challenge and reward systems are grounded in behavioral science research on habit formation and gamified motivation.

Overview

Project Overview

Between 2024 and 2026, the EarthStep research team ran a longitudinal study of 4,200 users to test whether gamification mechanics — XP, streaks, and visual feedback — meaningfully increase the durability of sustainable habits compared to static tracking apps.

The study combined in-app behavioral data with quarterly surveys, comparing a gamified cohort against a control group using a plain checklist version of the same challenges.

Objectives

  • Measure habit retention over 30/60/90-day windows
  • Quantify environmental impact per completed challenge
  • Identify which reward mechanics most improve consistency
  • Test whether visual feedback (the Virtual Earth) outperforms numeric-only tracking
Method

Methodology

A mixed-methods approach combining quantitative behavioral logging with periodic qualitative surveys across two matched cohorts.

Cohort design

4,200 participants split evenly between a gamified experience and a static-checklist control, matched for age, region, and baseline habits.

Data collection

In-app event logging captured every challenge attempt, completion, and skip, cross-referenced with quarterly self-report surveys.

Impact modeling

Each challenge type was assigned an estimated CO₂, water, and waste impact, verified against published environmental datasets.

Findings

Survey Results

Self-reported outcomes from the gamified cohort after 90 days, compared against the static-checklist control group.
